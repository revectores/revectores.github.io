Polynomials Factorization

Factor \(a^3+b^3+c^3-3abc\).

Observation. Notice that the polynomial is symmetric, we guess it contains \((a+b+c)\), to generate \(a^3\), we need to provide \(a^2\), hence \((a^2+b^2+c^2)\) must be included due to the symmetry, but this also generate \(a^2b\), hence we need to substract a second-order symmetric polynomial \((ab+bc+ca)\) to remove the redundant term, hence we got \[ a^3+b^3+c^3 - 3abc = (a+b+c)(a^2+b^2+c^2-ab-bc-ca) \]
